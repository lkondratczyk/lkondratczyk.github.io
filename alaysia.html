What Self-Driving Cars Means for...

Insurance

  • The need for insurance will decrease because as we move into self-driving cars the liability shifts from driver to manufacturer of the vehicle, the software developer, and any third parties related.

  • “If nondiscretionary-communicative vehicles became mandatory (as the introduction of either the interactive or remote-controlled models might require), there would be no need for private insurance, since all liability for AVs would be transferred to manufacturers”

Licensing

  • The best part of turning 16, besides later curfews and getting halfway through high school is getting your license.

  • The US National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) has said that Google’s self-driving cars will now be considered legal drivers in the eyes of the law

  • At this point self-driving cars will need to have a licensed driver in the car, to manually drive the car in the event of an emergency, until self-driving cars are proven safe.

Courier and Semi

  • A recent study, shows that people are using courier services more than ever. With developments such as Postmates, Door Dash, and Taskrabbit people can hire other people to do just about anything on their smart devices.
